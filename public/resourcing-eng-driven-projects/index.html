Policy & Operation

  • 20% of product engineering team’s time can be devoted to eng-driven projects
  • those projects must have a written proposal and be reviewed by leadership, although they’ll generally be approved if the work is aligned with driving engineering quality
  • given the small allocation, these projects will be resourced first rather than last to avoid starving these projects
  • product engineers are responsible for writing and socializing these docs. if they don’t, then they won’t get their 20% time

Concretely, we are starting with two engineering-driven projects to test out this approach:

  • Improve content suggestions without requiring internal alignment via ML-driven content recommendations.
  • Decoupling R&D from releasing new content.

Diagnosis

  • hard to prioritize eng-driven projects because we have many other projects
  • hard to compare tech debt vs growth vs compliance risk though: e.g. an investment allocation is much more doable than stack ranking
